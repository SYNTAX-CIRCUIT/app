Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is UGC NET Computer Science and why should I clear it?
UGC NET CS is a national-level exam conducted by NTA. Clearing it makes you eligible for Assistant Professor positions in universities/colleges across India, and if you qualify for JRF, you receive a monthly fellowship of ₹37,000 for research. It's one of the most prestigious exams for CS post-graduates.
Is this course suitable for December 2026 attempt only?
This batch is specifically timed for the December 2026 cycle. However, all recordings, notes, and mock tests remain accessible — so if you plan for June 2027, you can continue using the material. The syllabus rarely changes between cycles.
What if I miss a live class?
Every live session is recorded and uploaded within 4 hours. You get lifetime access to all recordings. Plus, our Telegram channel posts summary notes of each class so you can catch up quickly.
Does this course cover Paper I as well?
Yes! The "Paper I + Paper II" plan includes 15 dedicated sessions for Paper I covering Teaching Aptitude, Research Methodology, Comprehension, Logical Reasoning, Data Interpretation, Communication, and Mathematical Reasoning.
How are mock tests conducted?
Mock tests are conducted on an online platform simulating the actual NTA interface. You get 3 hours (Paper I + II combined), and results with detailed explanations are released within 24 hours. We conduct 15 mocks — 5 unit-wise + 10 full-length.
What is the eligibility for UGC NET CS?
You need a Master's degree in Computer Science / Applications / IT / Electronics with at least 55% marks (50% for OBC-NCL/SC/ST/PwD). Final year post-graduate students are also eligible to appear.
Is there any negative marking in UGC NET?
No. There is no negative marking in either Paper I or Paper II of UGC NET. This makes attempted-question strategy very important — our faculty will teach you how to maximize your score safely.
